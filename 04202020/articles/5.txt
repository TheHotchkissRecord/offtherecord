The 2020 presidential election is only a few months away, and U.S. citizens will find themselves making a defining vote that will affect the future of the country. The U.S. presidential election is held through an electoral college system. This differs from a simple majority rule used by over half of the world’s democracies, including France and Mexico. Dr. Rachel Myers, director of diversity and inclusion, said, “It’s worth a ‘refresher’ about the difference between the popular vote and the Electoral College. We are a school community that is global and not just national. We shouldn’t assume that everyone in the community fully understands how the election process in the United States works.”
